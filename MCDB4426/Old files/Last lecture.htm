General ideas and concepts of the cell-cell signaling

Cell signaling: Mechanisms that one cell uses to communicate and influence the behavior of another cell. In a broader sense, the signaling could include environmental cues received by a cell such as smell, light, mechanic pressure, and heat etc.

Three ways of cell signaling

a.     long-ranch signaling by secreted molecules

b.     contact signaling by plasma-membrane-bound molecules

c.      contact signaling via GAP junctions

b. G protein-linked severn transmembrane receptors

c. Ion channel-linked receptors

d. Intracellular Receptors--Steriod hormone receptors

Second messengers

Definition: The small molecules that transduce external signals into internal signals and whose diffusion enables a signal to propagate rapidly throughout the cell.

          - cAMP

          -Ca 2+

          -IP3

          -DAG

Systems

1. Embryo development

a.     Mosaic Development (autonomous specification: determined by local determinants and no cell-cell interaction)

Experimental test: remove one blastomere, the blastomere still develops as it will be in the original embryo

b.     Regulative Development (conditional specificaiton: determined by cell-cell interaction and local determinants not important; e.g. mouse)

Experimental test: if remove one blastomere, the other blastomeres will take over and the embryo is fine.

c.     Intermediate (Xenopus, fly and C. elegans)

Advantage or disadvantage of various approaches

1. Genetics approach

Help systematically identify important components of a particular biological process and define the genetic relationships of these components in the pathway.  But genetics usually can not reveal the mechanisms underlining a biological process.

2. Biochemical approaches

Very effective in elucidating the detailed mechanisms underlining a biological process.  Can also help identify components of a biological process but usually can not reveal the whole picture and is restricted by the availability and relative quantity of materials, the experimental techniques or methods.
