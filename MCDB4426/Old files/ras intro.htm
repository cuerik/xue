1980, first two ras genes were identified: v-H-ras, Harvey sarcoma virus; v-K-ras, from Kristen sarcoma virus.

Then it was rediscovered in gene transfer assays.

ras mutations (ras oncogenes)

12, 13, 59, and 61 (Naturally occurring), Gly12Val

63, 116, and 119 (in vitro)

ras is one of the major causes of cancer -- 50% of tumors examined has mutation in ras gene.

Highly conserved molecule: present from yeast to human and are interchangeable

Bind GTP and GDP and is a GTPase

  1. anti-ras antibody that inhibits GTP binding reverses ras oncogene transforming phenotype
  2. ras mutants that lose their ability to bind GTP can not transform cells
  3. GTPase activity of ras gene is severly impaired in their transforming alleles.
